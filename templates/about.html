The Project

RuhhLab is my final year project for BCA in Data Science at Amity University. The idea came from my deep interest in Computer Vision and Artificial Intelligence — fields that I believe are reshaping how machines understand the world around them.

While studying computer vision systems, I explored multiple research papers and open-source projects, which inspired me to build my own intelligent image understanding system. Instead of relying on cloud APIs or heavyweight frameworks, I wanted to prove that production-grade AI pipelines could run entirely on a local machine.

The Journey

This journey was not easy. I faced multiple errors, dependency conflicts, deployment failures, and system crashes along the way. Getting YOLOv8n to run reliably in a CPU-only environment without bloating the build size was a challenge that required days of iteration and research.

However, I continued improving the project step by step — debugging, refactoring, testing, and refining. Every error taught me something new. Every crash made the final system more resilient.

What This Means

RuhhLab represents my learning journey, persistence, and passion for AI systems. Even when things broke — and they broke often — I never gave up. This project is proof that lightweight, production-ready AI doesn't require a data center or a GPU farm.

It's a system I built from scratch, understand completely, and am proud to put my name on.
